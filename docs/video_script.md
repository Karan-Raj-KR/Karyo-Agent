# KĀRYO Demo Video Script
### Target: 3–4 minutes | Format: MP4 / YouTube Unlisted / Loom

---

## Pre-Recording Setup Checklist

Complete these BEFORE hitting record:

- [ ] **Terminal font size:** Set to 18pt minimum (iTerm2: Preferences → Profiles → Text)
- [ ] **Screen resolution:** 1080p or higher
- [ ] **Cache warm:** Run `python agent.py --city "Indiranagar" --category "dentists"` once so all cache is populated
- [ ] **Cache-only mode:** Set `KARYO_CACHE_ONLY=1` in your shell — this makes the demo run in 5–8 seconds with zero network calls
- [ ] **Hide .env file:** Close any tabs/editors showing `.env`. Blur or close if visible in the file tree
- [ ] **Face-cam ready:** Good lighting (face the light source), neutral background, no backlighting
- [ ] **Audio:** Use a microphone or earphone mic — NOT laptop speakers picked up by mic
- [ ] **Notifications:** Turn on Do Not Disturb. Close Slack, email, browser notifications
- [ ] **Files open and ready:**
  - Terminal at the project root
  - `outputs/emails/devaki_dental_clinic.txt` open in a text editor
  - `outputs/leads.csv` open in Excel/Numbers or ready to `cat`
  - `docs/architecture.md` rendered at mermaid.live (screenshot saved)
- [ ] **Recording software:** OBS (system audio capture) or Loom (easiest, includes face-cam PiP)

## Script

---

### SEGMENT 1: THE HOOK [0:00 – 0:30] — Face-cam

**What to show:** Face-cam only. Confident, direct. No slides needed.

**Say:**
> "Hey — I'm Karan. I built KĀRYO: a multi-agent AI system that finds local businesses
> that need a website, scores them on how likely they are to convert, and writes personalized
> cold emails — fully autonomously.
>
> The problem? Digital agencies and freelancers spend hours manually searching Google Maps,
> checking websites, and writing pitches from scratch. KĀRYO does all of that in under
> 10 seconds with a single terminal command."

---

### SEGMENT 2: THE ARCHITECTURE [0:30 – 1:00] — Screen share

**What to show:** The Mermaid diagram (screenshot from mermaid.live or the architecture.md file)

**Say:**
> "There are four agents working in sequence. First, the Researcher discovers businesses via
> OpenStreetMap — free, no API key — and checks each one's website health and domain age.
>
> The Scorer evaluates each business on two dimensions: how broken their digital presence is,
> and how likely they are to pay for help.
>
> The Manager is the most novel part — it runs a 2-pass self-correction loop. Clear leads
> are approved or rejected instantly. Borderline leads get a research follow-up question
> generated by Groq, are re-analyzed, and re-scored at a lower threshold.
>
> Finally, the Copywriter writes 100-to-140 word personalized cold emails for the top 5 leads."

---

### SEGMENT 3: LIVE EXECUTION [1:00 – 3:00] — Screen share, terminal

**Before starting:** Make sure `KARYO_CACHE_ONLY=1` is set in your shell.

**Type in terminal:**
```bash
KARYO_CACHE_ONLY=1 python agent.py --city "Indiranagar" --category "dentists"
```

**Narrate as each section appears — keep it conversational:**

*When the KĀRYO banner appears:*
> "One command. City and category. That's all the input it needs."

*When Researcher fires:*
> "The Researcher is hitting OpenStreetMap right now — finding dentists in Indiranagar,
> checking their websites for SSL, speed, and whether they're even reachable. It builds
> a full dossier on each one."

*When Scorer fires:*
> "The Scorer sends each dossier to Groq's Llama 70B model with a precise rubric —
> presence gap score from 1 to 10, conversion likelihood from 1 to 10."

*When Manager Pass 1 appears with REJECT panels:*
> "The Manager's first pass. Anything scoring 8 out of 20 or below is auto-rejected.
> These businesses either have too small a gap or are unlikely to convert."

*When Manager Pass 2 appears with follow-up queries:*
> "Here's the self-correction loop. For borderline leads — scores between 9 and 15 —
> the Manager generates a targeted research question with Groq. Asks: what's the single
> most important thing to verify about this business? Then re-researches, re-scores,
> and approves at a lower threshold of 13 out of 20."

*When Copywriter fires and emails appear:*
> "The Copywriter generates a personalized email for each approved lead — 100 to 140 words,
> citing the specific gaps found. Word count is validated and retried automatically."

*When the final table appears:*
> "Five approved leads, ranked by score. Full audit trail in run_log.json."

**Total run time with warm cache:** ~5–8 seconds

---

### SEGMENT 4: SHOW THE OUTPUTS [3:00 – 3:30] — Screen share

**Step 1 — Open an email file:**

```bash
cat outputs/emails/devaki_dental_clinic.txt
```

**Say:**
> "This email was written autonomously. Subject line references the specific gap.
> The body cites zero reviews, no phone number, no website — all pulled from the dossier.
> Ends with a single call-to-action. 100 to 140 words, every time."

**Step 2 — Show leads.csv:**

```bash
cat outputs/leads.csv
```
or open in Excel/Numbers

**Say:**
> "And here's the CSV — name, address, score breakdown, primary gap, manager's reasoning.
> Ready to drop into any CRM or email tool."

---

### SEGMENT 5: IMPACT [3:30 – 4:00] — Face-cam

**Say:**
> "KĀRYO works for any city and any business category — dentists, gyms, restaurants,
> lawyers. OpenStreetMap has global coverage.
>
> A freelancer can run this every morning and have five warm, scored, pre-written leads
> ready before their first coffee. No spreadsheets. No copy-paste. No generic templates.
>
> The 2-pass Manager loop means you're not just getting a filtered list — you're getting
> leads that have been re-evaluated and verified. The audit trail in run_log.json shows
> every decision and why it was made.
>
> This is KĀRYO. Thanks for watching."
